STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE
Title:  Purchase-to-Pay (P2P) Supply Chain Process
Document ID: SCP-042
Version: 3.1
Owner: Supply Chain Operations Department
Approved by: Chief Procurement Officer
Effective Date: 2024-01-15
Review Cycle: Annual

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
1. PURPOSE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
This SOP defines the end-to-end purchase-to-pay process for all direct and indirect
procurement activities. It governs how purchase requisitions are raised, approved,
converted to purchase orders, fulfilled by suppliers, received at warehouses, and
settled through accounts payable.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
2. SCOPE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Applies to all business units that procure goods or services above £500. Excludes
petty cash purchases and emergency procurement governed by SCP-043.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
3. ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

3.1 Requestor
Any employee who identifies a business need and initiates a Purchase Requisition (PR)
in the ERP system (SAP S/4HANA). Responsible for providing accurate cost centre,
quantity, and specification data.

3.2 Line Manager
Must review and approve Purchase Requisitions up to £5,000 within two business days
of submission. Approval is performed in SAP S/4HANA via the Manager Self-Service portal.

3.3 Procurement Officer
Reviews all approved PRs above £500. Selects or confirms the preferred supplier from
the Approved Vendor List (AVL). Converts the PR to a Purchase Order (PO) in SAP S/4HANA.
Responsible for ensuring all suppliers are registered in the Supplier Portal before
a PO is issued.

3.4 Category Manager
Manages strategic supplier relationships for spend categories exceeding £50,000
annually. Leads competitive tendering (RFQ/RFP) processes and negotiates framework
agreements stored in the Contract Management System (CMS).

3.5 Warehouse Team
Responsible for physical receipt of goods. Performs a Goods Receipt (GR) in SAP
S/4HANA when items arrive, matching quantity and condition against the PO. Raises
a Discrepancy Report if goods are damaged or quantity differs.

3.6 Accounts Payable Team
Processes supplier invoices received via the Invoice Management System (IMS). Performs
three-way matching (PO vs GR vs Invoice) in SAP S/4HANA. Initiates payment run in
the Banking Platform on approved payment dates.

3.7 Finance Controller
Approves invoices exceeding £25,000 before payment is released. Monitors budget
compliance via the Finance Dashboard (Power BI).

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
4. PROCESS STEPS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Step 1 – Requisition Creation
The Requestor creates a Purchase Requisition in SAP S/4HANA, specifying item
description, quantity, required delivery date, and cost centre. The system
automatically checks budget availability against the Annual Budget Plan stored
in the Finance Module.

Step 2 – Requisition Approval
The PR is routed to the Line Manager for approval via automated workflow in
SAP S/4HANA. If the PR value exceeds £5,000, it is additionally routed to the
Procurement Officer for secondary approval. PRs above £50,000 require Category
Manager sign-off. Unactioned PRs trigger an automated escalation email after 48
hours, notifying the approver's manager.

Step 3 – Supplier Selection
The Procurement Officer checks the Approved Vendor List (AVL) maintained in the
Supplier Portal. For new suppliers or spend above £10,000 with a non-contracted
supplier, a Request for Quotation (RFQ) must be issued to a minimum of three
suppliers. RFQ responses are evaluated using the Supplier Scorecard stored in the
Procurement Analytics Dashboard.

Step 4 – Purchase Order Creation
Once a supplier is confirmed, the Procurement Officer converts the approved PR into
a Purchase Order in SAP S/4HANA. The PO is transmitted electronically to the supplier
via the Supplier Portal or EDI. A copy is automatically filed in the Document
Management System (DMS).

Step 5 – Order Acknowledgement
The supplier must acknowledge receipt of the PO within 24 hours via the Supplier
Portal. If no acknowledgement is received, the Procurement Officer is alerted by the
Supplier Portal's automated notification service. Persistent non-acknowledgement
(>48 hours) is escalated to the Category Manager.

Step 6 – Goods Receipt
Upon delivery, the Warehouse Team scans the delivery note and performs a Goods Receipt
transaction in SAP S/4HANA. The GR record is matched to the open PO. If a discrepancy
in quantity or quality is detected, a Discrepancy Report is logged in the Quality
Management Module and the Procurement Officer is notified to liaise with the supplier.

Step 7 – Invoice Processing
Suppliers submit invoices to the Invoice Management System (IMS) either via email or
the Supplier Portal. The IMS performs Optical Character Recognition (OCR) to extract
invoice data and creates a draft record. The Accounts Payable Team reviews the draft
and initiates three-way matching in SAP S/4HANA.

Step 8 – Three-Way Matching
SAP S/4HANA automatically compares the Purchase Order, Goods Receipt, and Invoice.
If all three documents match within defined tolerance levels (±2% or ±£50, whichever
is lower), the invoice is auto-approved. Mismatches are flagged for manual review
by the Accounts Payable Team. Invoice disputes are tracked in the IMS Dispute Register.

Step 9 – Payment
Approved invoices are included in the weekly payment run executed by the Accounts
Payable Team via the Banking Platform. Payment terms default to Net 30 days unless
a framework agreement specifies otherwise. Remittance advices are sent automatically
to suppliers via the Supplier Portal.

Step 10 – Reconciliation and Reporting
The Finance Controller reconciles AP ledger balances monthly using the Finance Dashboard
in Power BI. Outstanding POs, overdue invoices, and budget variances are reported to
the CFO as part of the Monthly Management Accounts pack.

5. KEY POLICIES AND CONTROLS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

5.1 Segregation of Duties Policy
No single individual may create a PR, approve it, convert it to a PO, and approve
the resulting invoice. SAP S/4HANA enforces role-based access control to prevent
conflicts.

5.2 Approved Vendor List (AVL) Policy
All suppliers must be onboarded through the Supplier Onboarding Process (SOP-SC-007)
before a PO can be raised. Onboarding includes credit checks, compliance screening,
and GDPR data processing agreements.

5.3 Procurement Thresholds Policy
Purchases below £500: no PR required.
£500–£5,000: Line Manager approval.
£5,001–£50,000: Procurement Officer approval.
Above £50,000: Category Manager approval + competitive tendering.

5.4 GDPR and Data Retention Policy
All procurement documents (PRs, POs, GRs, invoices) are retained for 7 years in
the Document Management System in compliance with HMRC requirements and GDPR Article 17.

6. SYSTEMS USED
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
- SAP S/4HANA         – Core ERP for PR, PO, GR, invoice, and payment processing
- Supplier Portal     – External-facing portal for supplier registration and communication
- Invoice Management System (IMS) – Invoice capture, OCR, and dispute management
- Contract Management System (CMS) – Storage of supplier contracts and framework agreements
- Procurement Analytics Dashboard – Spend analytics and supplier scorecards
- Finance Dashboard (Power BI)    – Budget monitoring and AP reporting
- Document Management System (DMS) – Archiving of all procurement documents
- Banking Platform    – Payment execution and bank reconciliation

7. KEY PERFORMANCE INDICATORS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
- PR-to-PO cycle time: target < 3 business days
- Invoice processing time: target < 5 business days from receipt
- Three-way match auto-approval rate: target > 85%
- On-time payment rate: target > 95%
- Supplier portal adoption rate: target > 90% of active suppliers
